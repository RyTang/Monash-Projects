Finding the Best Country

The data from this graph is a personal compilation of data from the sources used below. Thus, the data might not be reflective of the actual population data.

Each country will have a dedicated peformance index for the 3 factors: GDP, Poverty, and Unemployment. Each best performing country will have a 100% performance index for that factor and every other country will be a percentage of that country. For countries, that do not contain data for that specific year, they are replaced with the median value for that year.

Across the years, we can see that the United States claims its first place amongst all the countries in being the best country to live in closely followed behind by China and Japan.

How has Poverty been in Certain Countries?

What defines poverty rate here? Poverty Rate is defined as the rate of people that are living with an income below the a poverty line. For this dataset, it is the number of people that are living on less than $1.90 (International Dollars) a day. In other words, it shows the rate of those in extreme poverty.

In the listed countries, we can see that all of them experienced a steady decline throughout the years, with Indonesia having the most significant difference across the years; decreasing by roughly 18% from 2007 to 2020. However for Ecuador, it experienced a sudden increase of 3% in poverty
